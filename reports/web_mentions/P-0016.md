### Aroostook County Genealogical Society — Lydia, Wife of Sherebiah Lambert
- **URL:** https://ac-gs.org/lydia-wife-of-sherebiah-lambert/
- **Site:** Aroostook County Genealogical Society (ac-gs.org)
- **What it adds:** A dedicated research article investigating who Lydia, wife of Sherebiah Lambert Sr., actually was. The article concludes that the most likely candidate is **Lydia Greenleaf** (born c. 1722, York, Maine), not Lydia Hopkins — because both families were from York and later relocated to the same areas of Maine, and the birth years better fit the known children's birth window (1750–1762).
- **New facts (if any):**
  - Proposed identity: Lydia Greenleaf, born c. 1722, York, York, Maine
  - One candidate investigated and likely rejected: Lydia Hopkins (born 1737, daughter of Stephen & Mary Hopkins), whose dates were seen as less consistent
  - Children born between 1750 and 1762 (consistent with a c. 1722 mother rather than a 1738 mother)

## Discrepancies

- **Maiden name (Hopkins vs. Greenleaf):** The GPKG records Lydia's maiden name as Hopkins, but the Aroostook County Genealogical Society article argues that **Lydia Greenleaf** (c. 1722) is a stronger match than any Lydia Hopkins candidate. If the Greenleaf identification is correct, the GPKG primary name "(Hopkins) Lambert" would need revision. [https://ac-gs.org/lydia-wife-of-sherebiah-lambert/]
- **Birth year:** GPKG says **1738**; the Aroostook article's preferred candidate (Lydia Greenleaf) was born **c. 1722**; the FamilySearch Hopkins record is **1736**. All three differ.
